Why DTDT

You do the assigned work well.
But what about your own idea?

  • Since 2009, many students master given assignments — yet struggle to turn their own ideas into results.
  • When we create, we usually create for others, not for ourselves.
  • DTDT is a method to develop your own design theory. Why not write your own book?

The Four Elements
Dream drives you to study Theory, shape a Design, and pick the Tool to build it. The point is not the four boxes — it is the logic that links them.
D · Dream

The energy source.

  • A dream is the source of the power to act and think — it makes you create, build theory, and learn tools.
  • Not a killer dream. Not only a dream. One actionable dream you can design with tools and explain with theory.
  • It need not be the best. It must be truly yours — the one that makes everything else worth doing.
T · Theory

Your own logic.

  • The bridge between Dream and Design — a theory for both, not for one alone.
  • It can borrow from an art movement, an artist's style, a science, a technology. But originality lives here.
  • Others may share your Dream and Design — your Theory and Tool make you different. Back it with evidence and examples.
D · Design

The outcome.

  • The example that embodies your concept — from the intangible (an idea) to the tangible (a site, a film, a product).
  • Many designs are usually needed before one converges. Theme → Concept → Result.
  • Without a strong Dream and Theory, a Design becomes a house built on sand.
T · Tool

How you make it.

  • A device used to produce an outcome but not consumed in the process — software, hardware, materials, methodology.
  • The tool can change the Design — even the Theory. Core Tool + design-specific tools.
  • What matters is not the tool itself but the method: Conventional → Computational. Your style, coded.
What stays · What moves
Dream & Theory stay constant — they are who you are. Design & Tool change with the client, budget, culture, and shifting technology.
The Core
The important thing is to make a logic
by linking the dream, theory, design, and tool.
It is a wheel

Keep turning.

  • Each element can be a whole study on its own — but they connect.
  • Run one full loop, get feedback, and the result feeds your next Dream.
  • The model itself has value — for a project, a business, or research.

DTDT Talk · Presenting

Speak at the audience's level.

If the audience does not understand, it is not because they are slow. A selfish talk demands they keep up. A good talk is designed so even a seven-year-old can follow.
DTDT Talk · 4 minutes

Five moves of a short speech.

  • Define yourself — jot down key words for who you are, your strengths and skills.
  • Hook your listener — not "I graduated in CS," but "I spent four years preparing to redefine how we use a computer."
  • Less is more — get to the point. Convey your message in as few words as possible.
  • Explain in layman's terms — don't impress with jargon.
  • Practice, practice, practice — in front of the mirror; polish your body language.
How you are evaluated

Peer review — two axes.

On presentation day, every classmate signs in and rates each speaker. The professor rates everyone too. Each card gets two star ratings plus one line of feedback.
★ Theory — foundation · precedent · timing ★ Design — visual + clarity
Dream and Tool are personal and contextual — they are not scored. You cannot rate your own card. Ratings and comments are visible only to the professor.
